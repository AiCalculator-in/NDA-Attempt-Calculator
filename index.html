What is the NDA Exam?

The National Defence Academy (NDA) examination is a prestigious competitive exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) twice a year. This exam serves as the gateway for young aspirants to join the Indian Armed Forces as officers in the Army, Navy, and Air Force.

The NDA exam is held in two phases annually - NDA 1 (usually in April) and NDA 2 (usually in September/October). Successful candidates undergo rigorous training at the National Defence Academy in Pune, one of the world's finest military academies.

Why is Age Eligibility Important?

Age eligibility is crucial for NDA aspirants as it determines how many attempts you have at this prestigious examination. The Indian government has set specific age limits based on different caste categories to ensure fair representation and opportunities for all sections of society.

Age Limits by Category

  • General General Category: Maximum age of 18 years (born not earlier than 2nd July of the exam year minus 19 and not later than 1st July of the exam year minus 16)
  • EWS EWS Category: Maximum age of 18 years (same as General category)
  • OBC OBC Category: Maximum age of 19.5 years (additional 1.5 years relaxation)
  • SC SC Category: Maximum age of 21 years (additional 3 years relaxation)
  • ST ST Category: Maximum age of 21 years (additional 3 years relaxation)
